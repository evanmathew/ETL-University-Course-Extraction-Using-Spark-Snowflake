On April 5, 2024, the University of Northern Valley released the Spring 2024 course catalog. A standout course is "Quantum Computing" (PHYS-410), taught by Dr. Karen Mitchell, scheduled for Mondays from 1:00 PM to 4:00 PM in Room 303 of the Science Building. This course explores quantum algorithms, computational models, and quantum information theory.

Enrollment for PHYS-410 began at 9:00 AM on April 10, 2024. Within 90 minutes, the course reached its capacity of 80 students. Dr. Mitchell explained that the course will include a final project (40% of the grade), a mid-term exam (30%), and weekly problem sets (30%).

In the Social Sciences Department, "Global Political Economy" (POLI-220) will be taught by Dr. Robert Evans. The course is set for Tuesdays and Thursdays from 11:00 AM to 12:30 PM in Room 210 of the Humanities Building. The course covers global trade, international finance, and development policies. Grading will consist of a term paper (35%), two exams (40%), and class discussions (25%).

The Health Sciences Department is offering a new course, "Public Health Policy" (PUBH-305), with Dr. Megan Wright. The class will be on Wednesdays from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM in Room 101 of the Health Sciences Building. Topics include healthcare systems, policy analysis, and epidemiology. Assessments include a policy proposal project (45%), a final exam (35%), and participation (20%).

Furthermore, the university announced a "Project Management Certification Program" starting in May 2024. The program includes six modules: Project Planning, Risk Management, Resource Management, Agile Methodologies, Stakeholder Communication, and Project Closure. Each module is two weeks long, with classes held on Tuesday and Thursday evenings from 6:00 PM to 8:00 PM. The program costs $2,800, and graduates will receive a certification endorsed by major corporations.

The registration deadline for the Spring 2024 courses and programs was April 20, 2024. The total number of students enrolled in the new courses and certification programs was 1,050, reflecting a strong demand for education in cutting-edge scientific and managerial disciplines.
