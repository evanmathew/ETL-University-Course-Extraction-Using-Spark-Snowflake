On January 10, 2024, the University of Southern Hills announced the Winter 2024 semester course offerings. Among the new courses, "Advanced Robotics" (MECH-520) led by Dr. Alan Parker, caught the attention of many engineering students. This course will be held on Tuesdays and Thursdays from 3:00 PM to 4:30 PM in Room 208 of the Technology Building. The course will delve into robotic kinematics, dynamics, and control systems.

Enrollment for MECH-520 opened at 8:00 AM on January 15, 2024. By 10:00 AM, 120 students had enrolled, reaching the maximum capacity. Dr. Parker mentioned that the course would include a group project worth 30% of the final grade, a mid-term exam (35%), and weekly labs (35%).

In the Arts Department, "Contemporary Art History" (ART-301), taught by Professor Linda Garcia, will be offered on Mondays and Wednesdays from 1:00 PM to 2:30 PM in Room 105 of the Arts Building. The course covers art movements from the 20th century to the present. The grading components include a research paper (40%), a final presentation (30%), and participation (30%).

The Business School introduced a new course, "Digital Marketing Strategies" (MKTG-410), with Professor Susan Lee. The course is scheduled on Fridays from 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM in Room 302 of the Business Center. Topics include SEO, content marketing, and social media strategies. The final grade will be based on a marketing plan project (50%), case studies (30%), and class participation (20%).

Additionally, the University launched a "Cybersecurity Certification Program" beginning in February 2024. This program includes five core modules: Introduction to Cybersecurity, Network Security, Ethical Hacking, Security Policies, and Incident Response. Each module lasts three weeks, with classes every Monday and Wednesday evening from 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM. The total program fee is $3,000. Upon completion, students will receive a certification recognized by leading tech companies.

The registration period for the Winter 2024 courses and programs closed on January 30, 2024. The university recorded a total enrollment of 900 students across all new offerings, demonstrating the growing interest in advanced technology and modern art studies.
